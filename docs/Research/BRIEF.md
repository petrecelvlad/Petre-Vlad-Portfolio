# Brief & Context

## Who this is for
Vlad Petre — game producer / designer, ~20 years in mobile games, spanning hyper-casual to midcore. Converting a static CV into an online portfolio.

## The original problem
Vlad had already built a first version: neo-brutalism + macOS-style window chrome (rounded corners, pastel pink/purple/teal, thick uniform outlines, playful traffic-light dots, toy-style icons). A senior UI/UX designer he trusted reviewed it and said it was **childish and doesn't do justice to his level of expertise**. Vlad agreed with the read but didn't know how to fix it, and was willing to rebuild from scratch.

## The designer's feedback, in full (translated from Romanian)
The designer said the goal splits into two separate problems:
1. **Find your personal visual voice** — something that "envelops everything like a membrane." His words: there's no wrong answer here, it just needs to be genuinely Vlad's, not a borrowed genre.
2. **Split the projects you've worked on into multiple layers/facets, so you speak the language of your future client.** — i.e. don't present one flat undifferentiated project description; different hiring audiences (producer-focused, designer-focused, economy-focused) need to be able to find the part relevant to them without doing translation work themselves.

Point 2 was solved quickly and well: split each project by "lens" (producer/designer/economy/etc), likely via tabs or a similar mechanism. Point 1 — the visual voice — is the actual unresolved problem this whole process has been circling.

## Audience & priority (confirmed)
First audience to land on the site: **recruiters, mostly non-technical**. For them, the aesthetic/visual polish makes the first impression and gets attributed to "professional and high quality" before any content is read. Content's job after that: make ~20 years of experience easy to grasp fast — via aggregate numbers (years, projects, teams managed) rather than requiring a full read of everything.

## What "done" looks like (working assumption, not confirmed)
A portfolio that: (a) reads as professional/senior on first visual impression to a non-technical recruiter, (b) makes 20 years of experience legible at a glance via aggregate stats, (c) lets different audiences (producer-hirers vs designer-hirers) self-serve the relevant facet of each project, (d) has a visual identity that is demonstrably Vlad's own rather than an assembled genre.
